## Slide 3 — The real cost

**Headline:** Manual workflows do not feel expensive — until you count what slips through.

**Bullets:**
- **Slow first response** → prospect calls the next company on Google
- **No follow-up cadence** → quotes sit until they go cold
- **Duplicate records** → team contacts the same person twice (or not at all)
- **Owner as router** → growth hits a ceiling at ~20–40 employees
- **Tool sprawl** → inbox + sheets + CRM + group chat, nothing agrees

**Quantified impact — sourced benchmarks + example math**

**Published research (cite on slide):**

| Finding | Stat | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| Slow B2B lead response | **42 hr** average response; **23%** never responded | HBR (2011), audit of **2,241** U.S. companies (Oldroyd, McElheran & Elkington) |
| Speed-to-qualify | **7×** more likely to qualify if contacted within **1 hour** vs. 1 hour later; **60×** vs. 24+ hours | Same HBR article (1.25M-lead follow-on study) |
| Follow-up persistence | **50%** of leads never get a **2nd** attempt; **93%** of conversions by **6th** attempt | Velocify Lead Response Management Study (~**3.5M** leads, 400+ companies, 2012) |
| Customer/admin paperwork cost | **$42.95/hr** imputed for customer/client record-keeping; owners handle it in **46%** of small firms | NFIB National Small Business Poll |
| Office labor baseline | **$46,320/yr** median (**~$22/hr**) | BLS OES, office & administrative support (May 2024) |
| Non-selling time | Reps spend **~70%** of time on non-selling work (**28–30%** selling) | Salesforce *State of Sales*, 6th ed. (2024, 5,500 sellers) |

**Example economics (label as scenario — customize in audit):**

| Input | Calculation | Result |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| 8 hrs/week on one manual workflow | 8 × 52 × **$42.95** (NFIB customer-admin rate) | **~$17,900/yr** time cost |
| +1 booked job/month | 1 × 12 × **$2,500** avg ticket (replace with theirs) | **~$30,000/yr** revenue (same lead volume) |
| 5 hrs/week saved after fix | 5 × 52 × $42.95 | **~$11,200/yr** recovered time |

**Bridge in:** “That pattern isn’t free — it shows up in the data.”

**Talk track:** Three beats — speed (HBR 42 hr / 7×), follow-up (Velocify 50% / 93%), time (NFIB $43/hr → ~$18K scenario). End with: **“Does that feel high, low, or about right?”** → *[Talking Points, Slide 3]*

## Appendix C — Savings calculator (use on calls)

Use **published rates** as defaults; replace with their inputs:

```
H  = hours/week on ONE workflow (ask on call)
R  = $42.95/hr  (NFIB imputed, customer/client admin) OR $22/hr (BLS median) OR their loaded rate
Annual time cost:  H × 52 × R

N  = quotes or inbound opportunities per month
J  = average job revenue ($)
L  = extra jobs won per month from follow-up fix (conservative: 0.5–1)
Annual revenue scenario:  L × 12 × J
```

- Velocify: 50% never get 2nd touch; 93% convert by 6th
- NFIB: $42.95/hr customer/client paperwork; 46% of owners handle it
- BLS: $46,320/yr admin median (May 2024)
- Salesforce 2024: ~70% of rep time is non-selling

**BLS anchors:** Office & admin support median **$46,320/yr** (May 2024, bls.gov/ooh). NFIB poll: **$48.72/hr** avg paperwork; **$42.95/hr** for customer/client records.
