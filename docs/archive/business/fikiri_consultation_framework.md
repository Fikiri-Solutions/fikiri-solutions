# Fikiri Solutions — Consultation Framework

**Purpose:** A repeatable, industry-agnostic system to diagnose how a business runs, find bottlenecks, and recommend automation—with clear next steps and pricing logic.

**Core model (every business):**

```
Input → Decision → Execution → Follow-Up → Money
```

- **Input** — Where work, leads, orders, or opportunities enter (any channel or feed).
- **Decision** — How they qualify, price, schedule, assign, or approve.
- **Execution** — How the team delivers the work (dispatch, job, visit, shipment, service).
- **Follow-Up** — Confirmations, reminders, exceptions, reviews, renewals.
- **Money** — Invoicing, payments, margin visibility, and whether the process produces predictable revenue.

---

## 1. Consultation call script (read word-for-word)

**Before you start:** Confirm who’s on the line (decision-maker + person who does the work). Block 45–60 minutes. Have a blank note doc open.

---

### Opening (2 minutes)

“Thanks for making time—I’m **[name]** with Fikiri Solutions.  

Today isn’t a sales pitch for random software. We’re going to map **one real workflow** in your business: how work comes in, how you decide what to do, how it gets done, and how money shows up at the end.  

That lets us see **where time leaks**, **where things break**, and **what’s realistic to automate** without making your team hate their jobs.  

I’ll ask a lot of questions; there are no wrong answers—honest beats polished.  

**One rule:** we focus on **one workflow** end-to-end. If other ideas come up, we’ll park them on a list so nothing gets lost.  

Sound good?  

Great—**which single workflow should we map today?** In one sentence, what’s the thread? For example: *new inquiry to first paid job*, or *posted load to covered driver*, or *new patient request to appointment on the calendar.*”

---

### Frame the workflow (2 minutes)

“Perfect. So today’s scope is: **[repeat their one-liner back to them].**  

I’ll walk through five beats: **input, decision, execution, follow-up, and money.**  

When I say ‘lead,’ you can translate that to **job, load, ticket, booking, order**—whatever matches how you talk.”

---

### Input (6–8 minutes)

“Let’s start at the top.  

**Where does this kind of work actually show up?** Email, phone, website form, text, portal, walk-in, partner feed—walk me through the real channels.  

**Roughly how many per week** in a normal season—not your craziest week, not dead week?  

**When something new arrives, where’s the first place it’s written down?** CRM, spreadsheet, dispatch board, inbox only, paper, group chat?  

**Is any of that logging automatic today, or is someone always copying and pasting?**”

---

### Decision (6–8 minutes)

“Once it’s in your world, **how do you decide go / no-go?** What makes you say yes, schedule, quote, assign a truck, take the job—versus pass or park it?  

**Is that criteria written anywhere, or mostly in someone’s head?**  

**Who has the authority to change price, timing, or assignment**—and where does that get recorded?  

**Where do weird cases go?** VIP, repeat customer, ‘we’ve never done this before.’”

---

### Execution (6–8 minutes)

“After the yes—**what are the concrete steps until the work is ‘done’ on your side?** Who does what, and in what tool?  

**What’s the biggest bottleneck today**—people waiting on each other, waiting on a system, waiting on a customer?  

**If the main person for this workflow is out sick for three days, what happens?** Honest answer is perfect.”

---

### Follow-up (5–7 minutes)

“After delivery—or between stages—**what do customers or partners hear from you?** Confirmations, reminders, status updates, ‘we’re on the way’?  

**How do no-shows, cancellations, delays, or rework get handled?** Is that the same every time or improvised?  

**Do you ask for reviews, referrals, or the next booking**—and is that automated, templated, or ad hoc?”

---

### Money (5–7 minutes)

“Let’s tie it to dollars.  

**How does this workflow end in money?** Invoice, card on file, COD, insurance billing, marketplace payout—however it works for you.  

**Do you know your rough conversion** from ‘thing entered the process’ to ‘we got paid’—even a guess?  

**Where do you look today** to see if this part of the business is healthy—report, gut, bank account?”

---

### Pain and tools (5 minutes)

“**What part of this process do you dread**—the step you’d delete if you could?  

**What tools are involved**—even if you hate them—CRM, POS, dispatch, sheets, industry portal?  

**Any rules we can’t ignore:** HIPAA, licensing, texting laws, brand tone, bilingual customers?”

---

### Close (3 minutes)

“Here’s what I heard back in **two sentences:** **[your summary].**  

The themes I’m noting are **[delay / manual work / leakage / risk / upside]**—we’ll turn that into a clear written recommendation.  

**Next step on our side:** we’ll send a short summary: the bottleneck we see, what we’d automate first, what it would take to build, and **ballpark investment** so you can decide fast.  

**On your side:** if this still feels like the right priority, we’ll book a follow-up to lock scope—or tell us what would need to change.  

**Anything we parked** that you want on the list before we wrap?”

## 2. Discovery question bank

Use what fits; skip what doesn’t. Translate **lead** → load, job, ticket, order, appointment as needed.

### Input

- Where do new opportunities **actually** arrive (not “where they should”)?
- Weekly volume in a **normal** week? Peak season?
- What’s the **first system** anything touches after the phone rings or the email lands?
- Duplicate entries: same customer/job in two places—how often?
- Anything only visible in **one person’s inbox** or DMs?
- External feeds: portals, load boards, OTAs, vendor catalogs—**who** monitors them and how often?

### Decision

- Go / no-go: **what are the 3–5 real criteria** (margin, geography, equipment, insurance, credit)?
- Are rules **written**, or tribal knowledge?
- Who can **override** price, timing, or assignment? How is that documented?
- How long does **qualification or triage** usually take? Worst case last month?
- Exceptions: VIP, partner referrals, complaints—**different path** or same queue?

### Execution

- Step-by-step from “approved” to “delivered” — **who + what tool** for each step?
- Biggest **wait state**: people, approvals, parts, external parties?
- Mobile vs desk: is the workflow **field-first**?
- Single point of failure: **one human** or **one login** that stops everything?
- What **proof** do you need to operate safely (rate con, scope sign-off, chart note)?

### Follow-Up

- What does the **customer** get automatically vs manually (confirmations, reminders, status)?
- No-show / late / cancellation — **playbook** or chaos?
- Internal handoffs: **how** does the next person know it’s their turn?
- Reviews, referrals, repeat bookings — **systematic** or when someone remembers?

### Money / ROI

- How does this thread **turn into cash** (invoice, POS, portal payout, subscription)?
- Rough **conversion** from entry to paid—or unknown?
- **Margin visibility**: do you know profit per job/load/visit, or only top-line?
- **Leakage**: discounts, refunds, write-offs, **unbilled** work—where does money slip?
- If response or assignment were **30% faster**, what would break less—or earn more?

### Current tools / digital infrastructure

- **System of record** for this workflow: one place or many?
- Integrations: what **talks** to what—reliably or duct tape?
- Identity and access: shared logins, **no admin**, former employee still on accounts?
- Data quality: can you export a clean list of **last 30 days** of this workflow?
- Change appetite: **open to switching tools** vs “automate around what we have”?

### Pain points

- What do you **dread** weekly?
- Last time something **fell through the cracks**—what happened?
- What would **definitely break** if the owner were gone a week?
- What does the team **complain about** that leadership already knows?
- Seasonal or event-driven spikes that **break the process** every year?

### Budget readiness

- Is automation **budgeted**, or exploratory?
- Typical appetite: **monthly** operational spend vs **one-time** project—preference?
- Who **signs** for software and services?
- Hard deadline (season start, contract renewal, hiring crunch)?
- Success definition in **90 days**: what would make them say “worth it”?

## 3. Diagnosis framework

After the call, label findings under five lenses. Use short notes; evidence beats jargon.

| Lens | What it means | What to look for | Example signals |
|------|----------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Delay** | Time where value waits—customer, cash, or capacity idle | Queues, slow first response, approval chains, batching that isn’t intentional | “Loads sit unassigned”; “callback next day”; “three people must approve” |
| **Leakage** | Work or revenue that **doesn’t complete** the intended path | Drop-offs, unbilled work, scope creep without change orders, no-shows without recovery | “Forgot to invoice”; “job closed in chat but not in system” |
| **Manual work** | Repeatable human steps machines do better at **consistency and speed** | Copy-paste, re-keying, dual entry, status meetings that exist only to sync | “Re-type from portal to sheet”; “screenshot to text thread” |
| **Risk / compliance** | Legal, safety, contractual, or reputational exposure | Wrong party on agreements, consent, licensing, audit trail gaps | “Price agreed in SMS not on file”; “PHI in personal inbox” |
| **Revenue opportunity** | Upside if the process were faster, clearer, or more consistent | Speed-to-quote, capacity utilization, upsell, repeat rate, margin on fastest movers | “First acceptable bid wins”; “30% no-show with empty waitlist” |

**Scoring (internal):** For each lens, note **Low / Med / High** impact and **evidence** (quote or fact). Prioritize fixes that hit **Delay + Leakage + Manual work** first unless **Risk** is High.

## 4. Pricing recommendation framework

Use this to **justify** a tier and fee band—not as a rigid calculator.

### Factors

| Factor | Questions | Effect on price |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Business size** | Headcount touching workflow? Locations? Franchise vs single owner? | More seats, handoffs, and governance → higher build + care |
| **Process complexity** | Branches, exceptions, multi-party approval, regulated data? | Each branch is design + test surface |
| **Lead / job volume** | Items per week through this workflow? Seasonality multiplier? | Volume drives integration depth, reliability needs, and support load |
| **Automation difficulty** | Clean APIs vs legacy portals vs PDFs vs phone-only? Need human-in-loop? | Fragile sources need monitoring, fallbacks, and iteration |
| **Urgency** | Fixed launch date, regulatory deadline, cash crunch? | Rush → phased premium or explicit tradeoffs (MVP first) |
| **Expected value** | Hours saved × loaded cost; revenue recovered; risk reduced | Anchor fees to **outcomes**, not hours spent coding |

### How to combine

1. **Start from expected value** (annual): hours saved + revenue lift + risk reduction—conservative.
2. **Add complexity markup**: integrations, exception paths, compliance, number of systems.
3. **Adjust for urgency**: fixed-price phases with dates, or deferred scope to phase 2.
4. **Set recurring care**: proportional to integration count, exception rate, and how business-critical the workflow is.

**Rule of thumb:** If you cannot explain **why** the price matches **value + complexity**, lower scope until you can.

## 5. Early-stage pricing tiers (Fikiri Solutions)

Calibrate dollar amounts to your market; structure stays.

### Tier A — Entry client

**Profile:** Single location or small team; **one primary system of record** (or willing to consolidate into one); workflow is mostly linear; **low regulatory** burden; volume in the **tens per week** not thousands.

**Typical scope:** One workflow; email/SMS + CRM or sheet + light scheduling; templates and routing; basic reporting.

| | Guidance |
|---|----------|
| **Setup / build** | Lower band; fixed scope; few integrations |
| **Monthly** | Light—monitoring + small tweak allowance |

---

### Tier B — Standard client

**Profile:** Multiple handoffs or **two systems** that must stay in sync; moderate exceptions; **hundreds of units per month** through the workflow; some **mobile/field** usage.

**Typical scope:** Orchestrated workflow with human approvals; portal or calendar sync; dedupe rules; dashboards someone actually opens.

| | Guidance |
|---|----------|
| **Setup / build** | Mid band; phased milestones |
| **Monthly** | Moderate—includes support SLA and periodic optimization |

---

### Tier C — Complex workflow client

**Profile:** **Many systems**, matchmaking or pricing logic, regulated or insured operations, high volume or **high cost of error**; needs strong audit trail, retries, and observability.

**Typical scope:** Multi-branch automation; custom normalization from messy inputs; role-based approvals; failure handling and runbooks; stakeholder training.

| | Guidance |
|---|----------|
| **Setup / build** | Upper band; discovery + build + stabilization explicit in SOW |
| **Monthly** | Higher—platform care, incident response, agreed change budget |

**Note:** “Complex” is not a badge—it means **scope must be smaller per phase** or **timeline longer**. Prefer **phase 1 = narrow slice that proves value**.
